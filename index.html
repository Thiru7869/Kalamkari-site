Viswanth Reddy

National Award Winner

Mannasamudram Viswanath Reddy, coming from a family of artists, carries a heritage of creativity that spans generations. His father, the late M. Munireddy, a well-known artist, underwent training under the Government from 1964 to 1966, giving him a deep understanding of traditional art techniques. Notably, Munireddy's father, who received the prestigious Tamil Nadu State Award in 1998, further boosted the family's reputation in the world of art.

Born on June 1st, 1986, to parents with a strong artistic spirit, Rajeshwari and the late M. Munireddy, Mannasamudram Viswanath Reddy was meant for greatness. Raised with his siblings, the learned elder sister, Ms. Munirathnamma, and the talented younger sibling, Vijaya Lakshmi, Viswanath developed a deep love for the arts from a young age.

Viswanath's studies led him to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree, which served as the base for his successful career. Starting in the field of Kalamkari artistry, Viswanath himself as a respected artist, earning awards that highlight his outstanding talent and dedication.

Viswanath's career is marked by many prestigious awards, including the Kamaladevi Puraskar in 2001, received in New Delhi. Other awards like the Kalamkari National Award in 2008 and the Kalamani Award in 2011 show his strong commitment to excellence.

Read more..

A major highlight of Viswanath's career was his receiving the Kalamkari Vaibhava Rathna Puraskar in 2023, a top achievement that confirmed his status as a master artist.

Key to Viswanath's story is his leadership of Sri Vijaya Lakshmi Fine Kalamkari Fine Arts, a respected institution started by his father and further grown under his skilled guidance. The organization focuses on the fine craftsmanship of sarees, carefully hand-drawn and colored with natural dyes, showing Viswanath's strong dedication to preserving traditional craftsmanship.

Wanting to spread his artistic work beyond India and all over the world, Viswanath went on a significant trip to London, taking part in an important Kalamkari workshop in 2010. For 40 days in the town of Stroud, Viswanath and his siblings engaged in a rich exchange of artistic ideas, broadening their artistic views and skills.

Viswanath's strong spirit and dedication inspire young artists everywhere, showing the lasting heritage of artistic excellence that defines the Reddy family. Driven by his quest for perfection, Viswanath shines as a beacon of creativity, lighting up the world of Kalamkari artistry.

Kalamkari sarees are made using natural methods. First, the cloth is soaked in cow dung and water to remove any dirt. This makes the fabric soft and ready for dyeing. Then, the cloth is washed with flowing water and dried in the sun.

After drying, the designs are hand-drawn using natural dyes. Artists use a bamboo pen and black dye made from jaggery and iron water to outline the designs. Each color is filled one at a time using natural pigments.
